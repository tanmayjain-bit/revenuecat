# Track 7 — Proof & Credibility

**Job:** Give the page substance, not just claims. RevenueCat sells on scale and customer evidence. Every stat below carries an inline source, date, source type, and confidence. The honesty bar (brief) is explicit: nothing fabricated; vendor-only and unverified claims are flagged.

> **Research note (June 2026):** Figures were sourced via **WebSearch + WebFetch** against RevenueCat's own properties and its *State of Subscription Apps* report, then every claim was re-fetched from RC's live pages with **Perplexity MCP** used for non-vendor corroboration; the resulting confidence state is recorded in the [Verification status](#verification-status-confidence-tagged) section at the end. Source-type legend: **PRIMARY** = RevenueCat's own data (note: vendor-published); **SECONDARY** = aggregator/independent press; **PRESS** = journalism. Confidence: **HIGH** = primary + recent; **MEDIUM** = reputable secondary / vendor self-report without disclosed methodology; **LOW** = undated/soft.

- **Benchmark transfer risk** — the State of Subscription Apps figures are real and well-sourced *as category benchmarks*, but a single app's results will vary. Use them as "across the category…" framing, never as a promise of a specific app's result.
- **G2/Capterra 4.8/5** — rating captured but review counts were not; lower-priority trust signal.
- **Non-vendor corroboration** — Perplexity surfaced **SaaStr**, an independent media outlet (though an early RC investor — not fully arms-length), repeating the 115K-app / $16B / 1B-transaction / "50%+ of mobile subscription apps" figures. This is corroboration that the *numbers are publicly circulated as benchmarks*, **not** an independent audit of RC's footprint. No third-party re-measurement of RC's scale exists in public coverage; the scale figures remain vendor-self-reported.
