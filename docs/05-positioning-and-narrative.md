## The one idea

**Drive revenue at product speed.** RevenueCat is the one place a PM operates the whole revenue lever — ship changes on your cadence, trust every number it produces, and decide on what's coming next.

## Value props

- **Primary — the umbrella:** **Drive revenue at product speed.** Ship, measure, and improve monetization on your own cadence — the revenue lever you own the outcomes for, now yours to move.
- **Secondary (in order):**
  1. **Velocity** — *the freedom to move.* **Move your revenue, not your roadmap.** Ship pricing and packaging changes the moment you decide to — not next release.
  2. **Source of truth** — *the confidence to quote a number.* **Quote every figure with confidence.** *Charts* gives you one true view across iOS, Android, and web.
  3. **Forward-looking** — *the conviction to bet early.* **Know the 12-month winner in week one.** *Experiments* surface the predicted-LTV winner mid-test — long-term revenue impact, not just short-term conversion.
  4. **Build-vs-buy** — *the focus to build product.* **Keep the upside, skip the plumbing.** Receipts, refunds, and renewals are solved — paywalls and *Web Billing* come free on top.

## Audience
Monetization/Growth PM (owns conversion/ARPU/churn/LTV/ARR, partners with eng, blocked by **release latency**). Must land for **enterprise** (reliability, governance, marquee logos) and **SMB** (speed, free tier). Lead on the common ground.

## Goal
Primary CTA **Start for free**; secondary **Talk to sales**. The page makes a PM believe *they* can own the revenue lever on RevenueCat — and that building it in-house is the slower, riskier path.

## The 3 pillars (each = a PM job, features are evidence)

> **Sub-note:** the "Evidence" column lists only the features that *fit* the job — not the full set. Each pillar carries the 2–3 strongest proofs of its claim; everything else is deliberately cut (see cut-list). Synthesize, don't enumerate.

| Pillar | The job | Evidence | Lead proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Velocity** | Hit your number without waiting on a release | Paywalls (no-code, AI, Figma) · Funnels · Targeting | Pixelcut +16% from one A/B test · top quartile +80% MRR vs bottom −33% |
| **2. Source of truth** | Quote your numbers with confidence | Entitlement substrate (cross-platform + Web Billing) · Automated Refund Handler · 40+ integrations · Charts API | 40+ integrations · *"I barely look at the platform dashboards"* |
| **3. Forward-looking** | Decide earlier, on what's going to happen | Experiments + predicted 12-mo LTV winners · LTV Prediction · paywall-perf charts | predicted-LTV winner · 55% of 3-day-trial cancels happen Day 0 |

**Underneath all three — build-vs-buy** (an objection, not a 4th pillar): concede the fee/"I could build it," then reframe via correctness (grace periods, build-it-twice, maintain forever) + opportunity cost. *Floga "3x the time."* The differentiating layer is free on top of billing you'd build anyway.

## Narrative arc
**Problem → shift → Pillar 1 (velocity) → Pillar 2 (source of truth) → Pillar 3 (forward-looking) → build-vs-buy → scale/trust → close.** Velocity leads. Emotional open (relief) → logical close (the number). Full section spec in [`site/page-scaffold.md`](../site/page-scaffold.md).
